Does Anatomical Proximity Predict Survival Model Transferability Across HPB Cancer Domains?

Introduction

Rare cancers defined by anatomical subsite often lack sufficient data for stable survival prediction. Cross-organ transfer learning can borrow prognostic information from larger, related cancer cohorts, but source selection remains largely empirical. We hypothesized that organ-level anatomical proximity provides a principled prior for quantifying cross-organ transferability.

Methods

Data. SEER 21 registries, 2013–2017 (n=67,821). Six HPB domains: HCC, ICC, gallbladder, and pancreatic head/body/tail adenocarcinoma.
